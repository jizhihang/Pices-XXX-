8  /**
9  *@class SipperBuff4Bit
10  *@brief Implements the KKLSC (Kurt Kramer Line-Scanner 4biit format).
11  *@details Coppied this code from the KKLSC library to allow the reading of 4bit scanner files.
12  *@code
13  * 1) Records are or varying length.
14  * 2) Each record starts with a 4 bit Op-Code.
15  * 3) Records contain either Text or Imagery
16  * 4) Imagery records can be eith Run-Length (Same Pixel repeating) or Raw-String(A sequence of pixels).
17  *
18  * Summary of Op-Codes:
19  * --------------------------------------------------------------------
20  * 0: End-Of-Line: Indicates that end of Scan-Line reached.
21  * 1: Text-Block:
22  * 2: InsrumentData
23  * 3: Un-Used
24  * 4: Run-Length (2 Pixels).
25  * 5: Run-Length (3 Pixels).
26  * 6: Run-Length (4 Pixels).
27  * 7: Run-Length (5 Pixels).
28  * 8: Run-Length (6 Pixels).
29  * 9: Run-Length (7 Pixels).
30  * 10: Run-Length (1 thru 256 Pixels).
31  * 11: Raw-String (1 Pixel Length).
32  * 12: Raw-String (Even length 2 thru 32).
33  * 13: Raw-String (Odd Length 1 thru 513).
34  * 14: Un-Used.
35  * 15: Un-Used.
36  *
37  *
38  * Bits
39  * =====
40  * 0 - 3: OP-Code (Values 0 thru 15)
41  *
42  *
43  *----------------------------------------------------------
44  * Op-Code = 0 end of line
45  * 4 - 7: Ignore
46  *
47  *
48  *----------------------------------------------------------
49  * Op-Code 1: Test Block.
50  * 4 - 4: End of text block.
51  * 5 - 7: TextBlock Len HighEnd.
52  * 8 - 15: TextBlock Len LowEnd
53  * Text-Block-Len = HighEnd * 256 + LowEnd.
54  * Followed by 'Text-Block-Len' bytes.
55  *
56  *
57  *----------------------------------------------------------
58  * Op-Code 2: InstrumentData
59  * 4 - 7: InstrumentId
60  * 8 - 39: Scan-Line Number.
61  * 40 - 71: Instrument Data.
62  *
63  *
64  *----------------------------------------------------------
65  * Op-Code = 4 thru 9: Short-Run-Len
66  * RunLen = OpCode - 2
67  * 4 - 7: Pixel Value being repeated.
68  *
69  *
70  *----------------------------------------------------------
71  * Op-Code = 10: Run-length of range "1" thru "256"
72  * Since there are no run-lengths of '0'; '0' will decode to Run-Lengrth = 1;
73  * 4 - 7: Pixel Value being repeated.
74  * 8 - 15: Runlen 0=1, 1=2, 2=3,... 254=255, 255=256
75  *
76  *
77  *----------------------------------------------------------
78  * Op-Code = 11: One Pixel Raw String.
79  * 4 - 7: PixelValue.
80  *
81  *
82  *----------------------------------------------------------
83  * Op-Code = 12 Raw Pixel String of even length ranging from 2 to 32
84  * because each byte can fit two pixels it is best that Raw String lengths
85  * always be multiples of 2.
86  *
87  * 4 - 7: Len; Num-Raw-Pixels = (Len + 1) * 2
88  * Len 0=(2 raw Pixels), 1=(4 raw pixels), 2=(6 raw pixels),,, 15=(32 raw pixles)
89  * Number of following bytes = (Len + 1).
90  *
91  *
92  *----------------------------------------------------------
93  * Op-Code = 13 Odd number of raw pixels up to length of 513 pixels.
94  * We use the other 4 bits in the 1st byte to represent the high order bits of the length
95  * and the 1st 4 bits ibn the second byte for the low order; that leaves room for one
96  * pixels in the second byte. This is why odd length stings are supported.
97  *
98  * 8 - 7: LenHigh String Length = 1 + 2 * (LenHigh * 16 + LenLow);
99  * 8 - 11: LenLow
100  * 12 - 15: First Raw Pixel. Will be followed by ((LenHigh * 16 + LenLow) bytes with two pixels
101  * in each one.
102  *
103  * Translation Tables
104  * ======================================
105  * 4 8 4
106  * Bit Bit 8-Bit Range Bit
107  * === === ============ ===
108  * 0 0 ( 0 - 15): 0
109  * 1 17 ( 16 - 31): 1
110  * 2 34 ( 32 - 47): 2
111  * 3 51 ( 48 - 63): 3
112  * 4 68 ( 64 - 79): 4
113  * 5 85 ( 80 - 95): 5
114  * 6 102 ( 96 - 111): 6
115  * 7 119 (112 - 127): 7
116  * 8 136 (128 - 143): 8
117  * 9 153 (144 - 159): 9
118  * 10 170 (160 - 175): 10
119  * 11 187 (176 - 191): 11
120  * 12 204 (192 - 207): 12
121  * 13 221 (208 - 223): 13
122  * 14 238 (224 - 239): 14
123  * 15 255 (240 - 255): 15
124  *
125  *@endcode
126  */
